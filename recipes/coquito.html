Recipe Coquito

Description

This Coquito recipe, a festive Puerto Rican coconut milk punch, is celebrated for its creamy texture and intense tropical flavor. The rich base is composed of coconut milk, cream of coconut, and a blend of sweetened condensed and evaporated milks, which provide its characteristic sweetness and density. It is flavored with traditional spices like cinnamon and cloves, along with vanilla extract, creating a warm flavor profile that perfectly complements the white rum.

The preparation is remarkably simple and efficient, essentially consisting of processing all the liquid ingredients and spices in a blender until smooth. Once blended, the drink is transferred to glass bottles and kept in the refrigerator. This chilling period is a fundamental step, not just to serve the drink cold, but to allow the spices and coconut flavors to meld and intensify before serving.

Ingredients

  • 2 cans (approx. 15 oz each) coconut milk
  • 1 can (approx. 15 oz) cream of coconut (e.g., Coco LÃ³pez)
  • 1 can (approx. 14 oz) sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 can (approx. 12 oz) evaporated milk
  • 1/2 cup white rum (or to taste)
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cloves

Steps

  1. Combine ingredients: In the container of a blender, combine the coconut milk, cream of coconut, sweetened condensed milk, and evaporated milk.
  2. Add flavors: Add the white rum, vanilla extract, ground cinnamon, and ground cloves to the milk mixture.
  3. Process: Cover and blend on high speed until the mixture is completely integrated and smooth.
  4. Bottle: Pour the mixture into clean glass bottles.
  5. Refrigerate: Place the bottles in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours (or overnight) to allow the flavors to develop and the drink to chill thoroughly.
  6. Serve: Shake the bottle well before serving, as the ingredients may naturally separate. Serve in small glasses and garnish with a sprinkle of cinnamon if desired.
